OKAZI SOUP

Description

Okazi soup is a thickened vegetable soup from eastern Nigeria. It is similar to Ofe Owerri, the main difference being that Ofe Owerri is thickened with cocoyam (Owerri court), while Okazi soup is thickened with achi, ukpo or ofo. Each thickener has its own peculiar flavour. Ofo is my favourite, while achi is my least favourite.

Ingredients

  • Meat
  • Stock fish
  • Dry fish
  • A cup of water + meat stock
  • 3/4 cups palm oil (3 cooking spoons)
  • Pepper
  • 4 tablespoons ofo
  • 6 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 3 Seasoning cubes
  • Salt
  • 60g achara (optional)
  • 40g fresh okazi
  • uziza leaf or seed (optional)

Steps

  1. Wash meat and stockfish, put in a pot with water, season with salt and seasoning cube, and boil.
  2. Wash dry fish: Soak dry fish in boiling hot water for 2 minutes, drain and rinse well with cold water.
  3. Add palm oil and pepper, cook till meat is tender.
  4. Stir in ofo very well. Whatever lumps that form will dissolve in the heat. Cook till the colour of the soup becomes homogenous, creamy and yellow.
  5. Add crayfish, achara and seasoning cubes, stir, taste and add salt. Cook for about 5 minutes on medium heat. Add okazi, stir and cook for 3 minutes.
  6. Take off the heat. Serve with swallow of choice.
